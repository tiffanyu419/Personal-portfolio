Swarthmore Student Government

Chair of Environmental Impact Committee | At-large Senator

The Swarthmore Student Government serves as the bridge between the student body and the administration. Our main responsibilities are to respond to campus wide situations, push up and discuss legislative bills written by students and serve as the student body representative on administration decisions. I was elected as an at-large senator my sophomore year and as the Chair of The Environmental Impact Committee my junior year by the entire student body. Below are some of the many projects that I worked on in the past two years.

FORUM ON DIVESTMENT AND SUSTAINABILITY

Mountain Justice is an activisim group that have been urging our college to divest since its founding in 2010. The administration has had many closed door conversations with Mountain Justice but both parties have failed to reach a consensus. In the recent years, Mountain Justice started two sit-ins and also passed a referedum through SGO, all of which the administration responded negatively to. With tensions running high, SGO decided to host a forum to bring this conversation to the whole campus, bringing in students and faculty with different opinions into a stagnant conversation. I invited an off campus, neutral regulator and collected and summarized questions from the whole student body, we created a platform where attending students could be educated about the issue and bring in new opinions and ideas into the conversation.

RESPONDING TO GLOBAL AND ON CAMPUS EVENTS

One of the responsibilities the student government has is to push bills and resond to on campus events. In the past year, we pushed a bill to change the credit non credit policy of our school and debated the usefulness of adding a diversity requirement into our curriculum. Other discussions we held included response to hate crimes committed on campus and the Divestment Referendum mentioned above.
